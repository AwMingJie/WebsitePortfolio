In this case study

ChallengeRole and scopeWorkflowOutcomeWhat it demonstrates

01 / Challenge

Connect monitoring to an end-to-end response workflow.

The simulation recreated the core flow of SOC work: monitor activity, identify potential incidents, add threat-intelligence context, review logs, and coordinate a response.

02 / My role and scope

Technical analysis within a team simulation.

I configured SIEM monitoring, analysed threat-intelligence feeds and indicators of compromise, practised log analysis and incident-response procedures, and collaborated with group members on the simulated SOC workflow.

03 / Workflow

Monitor, enrich, analyse, and escalate.

  • Used SIEM tooling to monitor network traffic and potential security incidents.
  • Analysed threat-intelligence feeds to identify indicators of compromise.
  • Practised log analysis, escalation, and incident-response procedures.
  • Collaborated with group members to simulate real-world SOC workflows.

04 / Outcome

One connected module exercise.

The simulation connected monitoring, threat-intelligence analysis, log review, and team-based response in one end-to-end exercise.

Evidence boundary

The public summary deliberately refers to the platform as SIEM tooling. The supplied project record does not establish which specific SIEM product was used for this simulation or provide quantitative detection results.

05 / What it demonstrates

SOC work is both analytical and collaborative.

The project demonstrates monitoring, threat-context analysis, log review, escalation discipline, and teamwork. Effective SOC work depends on clear communication as much as the monitoring tool itself.
